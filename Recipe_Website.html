Veg Biryani

Ingridients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes, bell peppers)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • A pinch of saffron strands
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • A handful of mint leaves, chopped
  • A handful of cilantro leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cashews, fried
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • Whole spices (2 bay leaves, 4-5 cloves, 4-5 peppercorns, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 2-3 cardamom pods)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste


Instructions

1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% cooked (it should still have a slight bite to it). Drain the rice and set it aside. 2. Prepare Saffron Milk: Warm the milk in a small saucepan over low heat. Add the saffron strands to the warm milk. Let it infuse for a few minutes until the milk turns golden yellow. 3. Prepare the Vegetables: Peel and chop the vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil in a large skillet or pot. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté them for 5-7 minutes until they are partially cooked. Remove the vegetables from the skillet and set them aside. 4.Prepare the Biryani Masala: In the same skillet or pot, heat the remaining ghee or oil. Add the whole spices (bay leaves, cloves, peppercorns, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods) and cumin seeds. Sauté for a minute until the spices become fragrant. Add the sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown. Stir in the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Cook for another 2 minutes. 5. Layering the Biryani: Reduce the heat to low. Spread half of the partially cooked rice evenly over the onion mixture in the pot. Sprinkle half of the chopped tomatoes over the rice. Spread half of the sautéed vegetables over the tomatoes. Sprinkle half of the chopped mint leaves and cilantro leaves over the vegetables. Drizzle half of the yogurt evenly over the vegetables. Repeat the layers with the remaining rice, tomatoes, vegetables, mint leaves, cilantro leaves, and yogurt. 6. Final Assembly: Pour the saffron milk over the assembled biryani. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Cook the biryani on low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice and vegetables are fully cooked and aromatic. Once done, remove the pot from the heat and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before removing the lid. Garnish the biryani with fried cashews before serving. 7. Serving: Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or any side of your choice. "Enjoy your flavorful and aromatic Vegetable Biryani!""

  • Calories: Approximately 350 kcal
  • Protein: 8 grams
  • Carbohydrates: 60 grams
  • Fat: 8 grams

Chocolate Peanut Butter Avocado Pudding

Ingridients

  • Fresh tomatoes (preferably ripe and firm)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze or balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste


Instructions

Prepare the Ingredients: Wash the tomatoes and pat them dry with a paper towel. Slice the tomatoes into ¼ inch thick slices. Similarly, slice the mozzarella cheese into similar-sized slices. Pick fresh basil leaves from the stems and wash them gently. You can leave them whole or tear them into smaller pieces if preferred. Assemble the Salad: On a serving platter or individual plates, start by layering the tomato slices. Place a slice of fresh mozzarella on top of each tomato slice. You can alternate between tomato and mozzarella slices for a visually appealing presentation. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the tomato and mozzarella slices, distributing them evenly across the plate. Season the Salad: Drizzle extra virgin olive oil over the assembled salad. Use a good quality olive oil for the best flavor. If desired, sprinkle a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper over the salad to enhance the flavors. Remember that mozzarella can be slightly salty, so adjust the seasoning accordingly. Optional Garnish: For an extra touch of sweetness and acidity, you can drizzle balsamic glaze or balsamic vinegar over the salad just before serving. This step is optional but adds depth of flavor to the dish. Serve: Once assembled and seasoned, the Caprese Salad is ready to be served immediately. Serve it as a refreshing appetizer or side dish alongside crusty bread or as a light accompaniment to your main course. Tips: Use ripe but firm tomatoes for the best texture and flavor. Similarly, opt for fresh, high-quality mozzarella cheese for the authentic taste of Caprese Salad. You can customize the salad by adding additional ingredients like olives, pine nuts, or a drizzle of pesto sauce for added flavor variation. Prepare the salad just before serving to maintain its freshness and vibrant colors. Enjoy your delicious and refreshing Caprese Salad!

  • Calories: Approximately 250 kcal
  • Protein: 5 grams
  • Carbohydrates: Around 20 grams
  • Fat: 18 grams
